Online Guides

While some mods have a guidebook to use (and most mods 1.16 and above have one), you may find yourself in need of an online wiki to use.
Before immediately going for a wiki, make sure to check if your mod already has a guidebook. Some mods, such as 1.16 Thermal Expansion, require the use of Patchouli.
Patchouli allows for mods to create guidebooks off of it, rather than having to code their own.


FTBWiki.org catalogues most mods relevant in 1.7.10.
Most major mods are fully fleshed out, such as Botania, Witchery, Thaumcraft 4 and Railcraft.
FTBWiki serves very handy for its version, as not all mods have a guidebook, or just need a quick reference for certain structures.


FTBWiki (but on fandom) primarily focuses on 1.12 mods, though mods on other versions may be on the site as well.
Similar in function to FTBWiki.org, only differing in content.

Individual Mod Sites

While both wikis serve well for a general range of mods, there are other wikis tailored to a specific mod. Non-fandom sites are usually created by the mod auther themselves.


Create
Contains exhaustive lists of the blocks and recipes Create has. Even the breaking times of blocks are shown!


Mekanism
Contains all blocks, items and recipes Mekanism adds, with a few tutorials for the mod as well.


Industrialcraft 2
Contains all items, blocks, recipes, etc. Some pages have flavour text sorely lacking on other wikis. The website has been filled by spam pages by bots, rendering the [Random Page] button worthless.


Witchery
All items, rituals, recipes, etc. available. Diagrams for recipes displayed, though the site could use less bright colours.


TeamCoFH
Official CoFH site containing documentation for everything Thermal on 1.12, with a few other pages for 1.16. Includes a page that displays all their mods, with downloads included.


Botania
The official site for Botania. While not exactly a wiki, the Lexicon tab allows you to view the in-game guidebook online. It'll be better to use the ingame guidebook, as it'll be tailored to the version you're using specifically.
